About Composting

What is Composting?

Composting is taking waste food (such as banana peels and egg shells) that cannot be eaten and converting it into usable fertiliser.

This shows how the cycle of food waste, compost and plant matter works in a simplified digram.

Why Should We Compost?

We can use useless waste to be converted into usable plant food. This reduces a large portion of trash, as well as help the growth of many plants.

This is a mind map of the possible outcomes of composting. A large benefit of composting is the reduction of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.
